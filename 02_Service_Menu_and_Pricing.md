## Pricing Philosophy

You have zero case studies and zero testimonials. Your existing business plan prices ($2,500 audit, $3-8K builds) are correct for where you'll be in 6 months, but they're wrong for right now. Here's why:

- A $2,500 audit from someone with no track record is a hard sell
- A $3,000+ agent build from a recent grad requires enormous trust
- SMB owners don't buy credentials — they buy proof that you've done this before

**The strategy:** Price low enough to be a no-brainer for your first 3-5 clients, deliver massive value, collect testimonials and case studies, then raise prices. You're not undervaluing yourself — you're investing in proof.

## PHASE 1 PRICING — First 3-5 Clients (Months 1-3)

Your goal: get in the door, do great work, get permission to use their results as case studies.

### 1. Free Workflow Audit (Lead Magnet)
- **Price:** $0
- **What they get:** 30-min discovery call + a 1-page summary of their top 3 automatable workflows with estimated time savings
- **What you get:** A relationship, intel on their business, and permission to propose paid work
- **Time investment:** ~2 hours total (call + write-up)
- **Why free:** This is your cold email hook. "I'll audit your workflows for free and show you where AI can save you 10+ hours a week." Zero risk for them.

### 2. AI Quick Win — Single Workflow Automation
- **Price:** $500 flat
- **What they get:** One workflow fully automated and deployed. Examples:
  - Lead response auto-drafter using AI
  - Email triage and prioritization bot
  - Document template generator
  - Meeting notes summarizer + action item tracker
  - Customer FAQ chatbot trained on their knowledge base
- **Deliverables:** Working automation + 15-min training video + 30 days of support
- **Why $500:** Low enough that an SMB owner can say yes without a committee. High enough that they take it seriously. You can deliver this in 4-8 hours of work using tools like n8n, Make, Lindy, or CustomGPT.
- **Upsell path:** "Now that you've seen what one automated workflow looks like, here are the other two I identified in the audit..."

### 3. Pilot Package — Full Workflow Overhaul
- **Price:** $1,500
- **What they get:** 3 workflows automated and integrated. Full documentation. 60 days of support. One revision round.
- **Deliverables:** Working automations + integration guide + training session + written case study (with their permission)
- **Why $1,500:** This is where you build your first real case studies. At this price, you need 3-4 of these to make rent while you're getting started. The case study is part of the deal — make this explicit upfront.

## PHASE 2 PRICING — With 3+ Case Studies (Months 3-6)

Once you have proof, raise prices. You now have before/after screenshots, time-saved numbers, and client quotes.

### 1. Workflow Audit & Roadmap
- **Price:** $1,500
- **What they get:** Deep-dive audit of all business workflows (not just the top 3). Prioritized automation roadmap with ROI projections for each. Tool recommendations. Written report.
- **Time investment:** ~8-12 hours (discovery + analysis + report)
- **Deliverable:** 5-10 page PDF report with workflow maps, automation opportunities ranked by ROI, and implementation timeline

### 2. AI Agent Build
- **Price:** $2,500 - $5,000 per agent (depending on complexity)
- **What they get:** Custom AI agent or automation built, tested, and deployed into their workflow
- **Pricing tiers:**
  - **Simple ($2,500):** Single-trigger automation, one integration, standard template. Examples: auto-responder, document generator, inbox sorter.
  - **Medium ($3,500):** Multi-step workflow, 2-3 integrations, custom logic. Examples: lead qualification pipeline, multi-channel customer support bot, automated reporting.
  - **Complex ($5,000):** Multi-agent system, 4+ integrations, custom training data, voice component. Examples: full sales outreach pipeline, AI phone receptionist, end-to-end onboarding automation.
- **All tiers include:** Deployment, training session, 30 days of support, documentation

### 3. Monthly Retainer
- **Price:** $750 - $2,000/month
- **What they get:** Ongoing optimization, new automations as needed, priority support, monthly check-in call
- **Tiers:**
  - **Maintain ($750/mo):** Up to 4 hours/month of support, monitoring, and minor adjustments
  - **Grow ($1,250/mo):** Up to 8 hours/month, includes 1 new automation or major update per month
  - **Scale ($2,000/mo):** Up to 15 hours/month, includes 2 new automations, quarterly strategy review, priority response

## How to Quote — The Proposal Template

When you send a proposal after a discovery call, use this structure:

```
PROPOSAL FOR [COMPANY NAME]
Prepared by Daniel Weadock | Monican
[Date]

THE PROBLEM
[Restate their pain in their words. Include the specific hours/week and cost.]

WHAT I RECOMMEND
[1-3 specific automations, described in plain English. No jargon.]

HOW IT WORKS
[Step-by-step: what I'll build, what tools I'll use, what they need to provide]

TIMELINE
[Usually 1-2 weeks for a Quick Win, 3-4 weeks for a Pilot]

INVESTMENT
[Price. Include what's included — support, training, revisions.]

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
[Clear call to action: "Reply to this email to get started" or "Book a 15-min call to finalize"]
```

Keep it to 1 page. SMB owners don't read long proposals.

## Pricing Objection Handling

**"That seems expensive."**
> "I get that. Let me put it this way — you told me [person] spends [X hours/week] on [task]. At their hourly rate, that's costing you [$Y/month]. This automation pays for itself in [Z] months, and then it's pure savings from there."

**"Can we start smaller?"**
> "Absolutely. The Quick Win at $500 is designed exactly for that. We pick one workflow, I automate it, and you see the results before committing to anything bigger."

**"I found someone cheaper on Upwork."**
> "You probably did. The difference is I'm not just building a bot — I'm analyzing your workflow, choosing the right tool, building it, training your team, and supporting it for 30 days. Most Upwork gigs end at delivery. Mine ends when it's actually working in your business."

**"Can you just do it for free and I'll pay you if it works?"**
> "The audit is free — that's how we figure out if this makes sense. But the build requires real time and tool costs on my end. The $500 Quick Win is the lowest-risk way to test this. If it doesn't save you time, I'll refund it."
