## Cold Call Script (if you end up calling)

> Ring ring...

"Hi, is this [First Name]? Hey [First Name], my name's Daniel Weadock, I'm calling from Bolton — I know this is out of the blue so I'll be quick.

I'm building a tech company called Monican, and what we're focused on is helping real estate professionals. But honestly, I'm early in this, and before I build anything I want to make sure I actually understand what people in your position deal with every day.

I'm not selling anything — I'm literally just trying to have conversations with people in the industry to learn. What takes up your time, what frustrates you, what you wish worked better.

Would you be open to grabbing a coffee sometime, or even just a 15-minute phone call? I'd really appreciate hearing your perspective."

> If they say yes: "That's awesome, I really appreciate it. What works best for you — morning, afternoon? And would you rather meet up somewhere or just do a quick call?"

> If they hesitate: "Totally understand. If it's easier I can send you my info by email and you can reach out whenever it works for you. No pressure at all."

> If they say no: "No worries at all, I appreciate your time. If you ever want to chat down the road, I'm always around. Have a great day."

## Notes for Daniel

- **Personalize every message.** Mention something specific about their agency. "I saw you've been in Harvard since 1947 — that's incredible" goes a long way.
- **Priority A people first.** These are the small independents who will actually pick up the phone and talk to you.
- **Track everything** in the Discovery_Outreach_List.xlsx — update Status, Notes, and Date Contacted after every touchpoint.
- **After the conversation**, write down the 3 biggest things you learned while it's fresh. That's gold for building the platform.
