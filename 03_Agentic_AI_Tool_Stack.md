## Why This Matters

When a prospect asks "what tools would you use?" you need to answer instantly and confidently. You don't need to know every tool — you need to know the RIGHT tool for each category and be able to explain why in one sentence.

Your job is not to be a tool expert. Your job is to be a workflow expert who knows which tool to reach for. Think of yourself as a contractor who knows which power tool to grab, not a power tool salesman.

## YOUR CORE STACK — Learn These First

These are the tools you should be able to demo, build with, and explain to a client on the spot. Master these before expanding.

### 1. n8n — Workflow Automation (Your Swiss Army Knife)
- **What it does:** Open-source workflow automation with 400+ integrations. Visual drag-and-drop builder. AI agent nodes for building LLM-powered workflows.
- **Pricing:** Self-hosted = FREE unlimited. Cloud: $24/mo (2,500 runs), $60/mo (10K runs).
- **Why you use it:** Self-hosted = zero marginal cost for clients. More powerful than Zapier for complex workflows. AI agent nodes mean you can build LLM chains visually. This is your primary build tool.
- **Affiliate:** 30% commission for 12 months on cloud referrals.
- **One-liner:** "It's like Zapier but more powerful and it can be free to run."
- **Learn:** n8n.io/docs, their YouTube channel, and build 5 workflows yourself.

### 2. Make.com — Workflow Automation (The Visual Alternative)
- **What it does:** Visual automation platform. More intuitive than n8n for non-technical users. Strong AI integrations.
- **Pricing:** Free (1,000 ops/mo), Core $9/mo, Pro $16/mo.
- **Why you use it:** When the client needs to manage automations themselves and isn't technical. Easier to hand off than n8n.
- **Affiliate:** 35% commission for 12 months. Best affiliate rate in the category.
- **One-liner:** "It's the easiest way to connect your tools and automate repetitive work."

### 3. CustomGPT.ai — Knowledge Base Chatbot
- **What it does:** Build AI chatbots trained on your business data. Upload docs, PDFs, website content. Deploy on website, in apps, or via API.
- **Pricing:** From $99/mo.
- **Why you use it:** Dead simple to set up. Perfect for any business with a knowledge base, FAQ, or support documentation. You can have a working chatbot in 30 minutes.
- **Affiliate:** 15-20% recurring for 2 years.
- **One-liner:** "Train an AI on everything your business knows, then let it answer questions 24/7."

### 4. Lindy AI — AI Agent Platform
- **What it does:** Pre-built and custom AI agents for email triage, meeting scheduling, CRM updates, customer support. Agents can coordinate with each other.
- **Pricing:** $8/mo starter, $50/mo pro (unlimited agents).
- **Why you use it:** Fastest path to a working AI agent for common business tasks. Multi-agent coordination is the real differentiator.
- **Affiliate:** Service Partner program with revenue share + lead referrals.
- **One-liner:** "Think of it as hiring a team of AI assistants that handle your admin work."

### 5. Claude (Anthropic) — Your AI Engine
- **What it does:** Large language model. You already know this. Use it for analysis, writing, code generation, data processing.
- **Pricing:** Pro $20/mo, Team $25/user/mo, Claude Code (what you're using now).
- **Why you use it:** It's your co-builder. You use Claude to analyze client workflows, draft proposals, build automations, write documentation. It's also what powers many of the agents you'll build.
- **One-liner:** "This is the brain behind most of what I build."

## THE DEMO STACK — What To Show In Meetings

When you're on a discovery call or follow-up and want to show what's possible, have these ready to pull up:

1. **A working n8n workflow** that does something visual (email comes in → AI categorizes it → routes to right person → drafts response). Build this once, screenshot it, use it in every meeting.

2. **A CustomGPT chatbot** trained on a sample business (use Kings Court or create a fake real estate agency). Show them: "This is what your website could do in 24 hours."

3. **A Lindy agent** doing email triage or meeting scheduling. Show the before (manual) and after (automated).

4. **A Make.com scenario** connecting 3 tools they probably use (e.g., Google Sheets → Gmail → Slack notification). Simple but relatable.

## TOOL EVALUATION FRAMEWORK — For New Tools

New AI tools launch weekly. When you encounter one, evaluate it with these 5 questions:

1. **Does it solve a problem I see repeatedly in client businesses?** (If no, skip it)
2. **Can I set it up in under 2 hours?** (If no, it's too complex for most SMBs)
3. **Does it have an API or integrate with n8n/Make/Zapier?** (If no, it's an island)
4. **What does it cost per month for a 25-person company?** (If over $500/mo, needs strong ROI justification)
5. **Does it have a partner/affiliate program?** (Nice to have, not required)
